Overseas Investor FAQ

Yes. Through a properly executed Power of Attorney (POA), you can authorise a trusted representative in Pakistan to complete property transactions on your behalf. Highmark Associates guides you through the entire POA process.
A POA is a legal document that authorises someone (your attorney) to act on your behalf in Pakistan. It must be prepared in Pakistan, authenticated by a Pakistani court or notary, and — if you are abroad — attested by the Pakistani Embassy or Consulate in your country, or notarised and apostilled as per requirements.
Our team conducts the full verification process locally in Islamabad on your behalf. We check CDA approvals, ownership records, NOCs, tax clearance, and provide you with a comprehensive verification report with supporting documentation.
Property payments can be made through official banking channels including telegraphic transfer (TT), Roshan Digital Account (RDA), or through authorised foreign exchange companies. We provide guidance on the most appropriate and legally compliant transfer method for your situation.
E-11, DHA Phase 2, and F-10/F-11 are most popular among overseas Pakistani investors due to clear documentation, strong rental demand, capital appreciation history, and CDA approval. Our team provides personalised investment advice based on your budget and goals.
